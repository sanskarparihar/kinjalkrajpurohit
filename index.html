Yes Its for you Kinjalk

I am just missing you so much buddy ❤️

So, Just a little corner of the internet for you, Some things don’t need a reason. This is one of them.

This isn’t meant to explain anything.
It’s just something I wanted to make — slowly, quietly.

I still remember the first day I saw you in our office. I knew someone was going to be transferred, but the day you walked in, I genuinely thought — so there are real humans in this office after all.

In my entire one year at Federal Bank, it was the first time I noticed someone so effortlessly beautiful.
And honestly, I’m not the kind of guy who walks up and starts conversations easily, especially not with someone who feels completely out of my league.
Yet somehow, I kept finding excuses — cases, files, work — just to have a few moments of conversation with you.

When we finally did talk properly, it made me question everything.
At that time, I was already serving my last two months’ notice period, with the option to revoke my resignation.
And I remember wondering whether leaving was the right decision at all.

Life, however, had its own plans.

Our entire department shifted to the Tilak Road branch.
And the day before the shift — the day we had a genuinely good conversation, even if it was about work — I remember feeling quietly happy. It was enough.

Tilak Road changed everything for me.

I chose to sit next to you for two reasons.
One — you were new, and I thought I could be of some help.
The other — because I wanted to.
That’s where things truly changed.

Those days, I felt happier than I ever had in my entire Federal Bank journey.

There was a day when you weren’t yourself — quiet, distant — and it genuinely affected me.
Not because you weren’t talking to me, but because the smile and joy that usually lived on your face were missing.
Even on my way home, I stopped just to text you an apology — not out of guilt, but out of concern.

Things got better after that.

When you went to Ujjain for a few days, I missed you more than I expected.
Those were my last days in the office, and I really wished you were around.
Still, the calls and messages during that time meant a lot to me.

The day you came back, I was genuinely happy — because I knew I’d get to see you before leaving.

And then there was that day — when we stepped out of the office, just the two of us.
No rush. No crowd. No noise.
That was the closest I ever felt to you, and it was truly beautiful.

“Gadbad teri bike ki seat mein nahi, kahin aur hai.”

Still stays with me. It made me smile in a way I didn’t expect.

Coming back to the office after four long hours felt funny, almost unreal.
Later, meeting you at your home a few times — those moments mattered too.

But honestly, those office days with you were the best part of my life.
I felt alive.

I’m writing this just to let you know — what we shared was real and beautiful.
And I truly believe there are many more good moments waiting for us ahead.

I miss you a lot, buddy.

Some meetings change nothing, some change everything,
You walked in quietly — and the world felt different.

Thanks for the gift though, i loved it ❤️.
